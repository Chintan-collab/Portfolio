Synthos - Harmonizing the Digital Symphony of PDF Insights

Synergizing Dynamic Data Extraction for Informed Insights via LLMs.

Brief Overview

This project explores the potential of a Retrieval Augmented Generation (RAG) architecture leading to dynamic data extraction from various documents. The main goal is to evaluate the capability of LLM (Large Language Model) to extract data dynamically using vector stores and diverse prompts. This innovative development has the potential to transform data extraction, enabling support for a broader range of documents with intricate structures that are traditionally challenging to extract.

Objectives:

Utilizing Large Language Models (LLM) for data extraction offers a transformative advantage over traditional tools like Tabula and Camelot. Unlike the rigid structure-dependent approach of Tabula and Camelot, LLM provides a dynamic and versatile solution for information retrieval. Rather than requiring a predefined document structure, LLM allows us to prompt and request data directly, regardless of the document's layout or format. This flexibility is particularly valuable as it eliminates the need for concise structure specifications, offering a more adaptive and efficient approach to data extraction from diverse documents.

Key Findings:

      High Computational Demands:
    • Developing and deploying this necessitates a robust, high-configured system capable of loading and inferring Large Language Models (LLMs), often exceeding 15 GB in size.
      In-Mmeory Vector Store:
    • In-Memory Vector Store: The essential requirement for this application is an in-memory vector store. This store becomes crucial as it facilitates the explicit provision of documents to the LLM Chain before inferring and extracting the required data for each extraction request.
      Varied Prompt:
    • Another critical aspect of our system involves the incorporation of varied prompts. To enhance the flexibility of data extraction, we require a diverse set of prompts tailored for different types of documents. This ensures that our system can effectively adapt and extract information from a wide range of documents with distinct structures and content formats.
      Hallucination and Information Accuracy Challenge:
    • Given our application's focus on precise information retrieval from multiple sources, there is a concern regarding hallucination by the Language Model. The LLM might generate responses based on inferred context, potentially leading to inaccurate or misleading information. Addressing and mitigating this challenge is crucial to ensure the reliability and trustworthiness of our application.
